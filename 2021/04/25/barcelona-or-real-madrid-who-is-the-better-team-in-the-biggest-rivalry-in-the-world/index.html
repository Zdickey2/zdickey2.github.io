2021-04-25

Recently named the two highest valued teams in the world, Barcelona and Real Madrid have a rivalry that is unparalleled to any other rivalry in any other sport. In the last 10 years the rivalry has brewed with many players joining and leaving including one of the greatest, Cristiano Ronaldo leaving in 2018.

So now to take a lot more stats into affect. Lets look at not only how they do offensively but also defensively. Finding a Zscore is a great way to look at who the best teams are because it looks at how far a team is from the average in a certain or multiple stats.

teamquality <- laliga %>% 
  group_by(HomeTeam) %>% 
  select(HomeTeam, FTHG, FTAG, HS, AS, HST, AST)

The biggest stats is soccer is goals (obviously), Shots and shots on target because the amounts of shots and shots on target because it shows how many Opportunities you give yourself. If you think about it though it does give you an idea of other stats; more shots means more possession, which means less Opp. possession, etc.

teamtotals <- teamquality %>% 
  group_by(HomeTeam) %>% 
  summarise(
    Goalaverage = mean(FTHG), 
    ShotAvg = mean(HS), 
    OppGoalAvg = mean(FTAG),
    OppShotAvg = mean(AS),
    STavg = mean(HST),
    OppSTavg = mean(AST)
    ) 
teamzscore <- teamtotals %>% 
  mutate(
    Goalzscore = as.numeric(scale(Goalaverage, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    ShotZscore = as.numeric(scale(ShotAvg, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    OppGoalscore = as.numeric(scale(OppGoalAvg, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)) * -1,
    OppShotScore = as.numeric(scale(OppShotAvg, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)) * -1,
    STzscore = as.numeric(scale(STavg, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)),
    OppSTzscore = as.numeric(scale(OppSTavg, center = TRUE, scale = TRUE)) * -1,
    TotalZscore = ShotZscore + OppGoalscore + OppShotScore + STzscore + OppSTzscore
  )

So the higher the zscore the farther away from the average in a positive aspect a team is.
